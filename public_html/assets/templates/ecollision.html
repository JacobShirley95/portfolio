What it is

ECollision is a basic elastic/inelastic collision simulation library that I made for my final year A Level project. It is designed to help students understand the laws of energy conservation and momentum by controlling the velocity and radius of particles in the simulation.

ECollision has gone through many iterations over the years since I left sixth-form. It was firstly written in ES5 JavaScript, but I then decided to move over to CoffeeScript, as this has better support for a class syntax. It is currently being ported to the ES6 version of JavaScript, which is now on par with CoffeeScript in features.

The rendering of the particles is done by a library called EaselJS. I chose to use StageGL instead of Stage as it uses the WebGL capabilities of the browser (hardware acceleration) to speed up the rendering porcess. I have also tried to keep the rendering and logic as separate of possible, meaning anyone can use this library headless as well.

Features

  • Separate logic and rendering
  • Hardware acceleration
  • Accurate physics behavior
  • Demos featuring a graph and other information about the simulation
